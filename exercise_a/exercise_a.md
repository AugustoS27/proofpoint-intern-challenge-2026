## 1. Describe the most relevant learnings from programming-related subjects during your career.

During my studies in Systems Engineering, I developed a progressive understanding of software development, starting with the fundamentals of programming and advancing toward the design and construction of more complex systems.

In the early stages of my degree, courses such as Algorithms and Data Structures were fundamental in developing logical thinking and the ability to break down complex problems into programmable solutions. Later, in Programming Paradigms, I broadened my perspective on different development approaches, especially the object-oriented paradigm, which allowed me to understand how to model real-world problems in software.

As I progressed through my degree, I delved deeper into application development and data management. In Databases, I acquired knowledge of relational modeling, normalization, and writing SQL queries, while in various projects I worked on application development integrating frontend and backend through REST APIs.

Finally, in courses related to Software Engineering and Systems Design, I incorporated concepts such as architecture, design patterns, SOLID principles, and agile methodologies, understanding the importance of building maintainable and scalable software. I also had my first exposure to modern development and deployment tools, such as Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD pipelines, and cloud services, which helped me understand how applications are managed in modern environments.

Overall, these experiences allowed me to build a solid foundation in programming and gain a broad understanding of the software lifecycle, from design to deployment.

## 2. Describe a situation you had to resolve with a teammate from a study or work group

During the Information Systems Design course, I participated in a group project that lasted throughout the academic year. The objective was to simulate the development of a real system based on a set of initial requirements representing the needs of a company.

The project began with the analysis of the requirements and the definition of a specific use case to be developed. From there, we documented the use case and modeled the solution using UML diagrams, mainly class and communication diagrams, to represent both the structure of the system and the interaction between its components.

Subsequently, we implemented the system using an architecture composed of a frontend, a backend, and a relational SQL database, integrating these components to support the defined use case.

Finally, the system was refactored to incorporate design patterns required by the course, which involved modifying the code structure to improve its organization, decoupling, and maintainability. This project allowed me to experience the different stages of software development in practice, from analysis and modeling to implementation and architectural improvement.
